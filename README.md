# 🧬 Immunology & Cancer Systems – Research Insights Portfolio

This repository summarizes key analytical and conceptual insights derived from work in tumor immunology, TCR repertoire analysis, and immune response modeling in cancer.

The focus is not only on data analysis outputs, but on **interpreting immune behavior as a system-level dynamic rather than isolated biomarkers**.

---

## 🔬 Core Research Insights

### 1. Tumor Mutation Burden vs Immune Activation (IL7R-mutant Melanoma)
- IL7R-mutant melanoma exhibits elevated tumor mutation burden (TMB).
- However, this is not accompanied by increased immune infiltration or activation signatures.
- Suggests a **decoupling between genomic instability and effective antitumor immune response**.
- Key implication: TMB alone is not a sufficient predictor of immune activity.

---

### 2. Multidimensional Nature of Immune Activation in HCC
- Observed partial uncoupling between cytotoxic activity and inflammatory signaling.
- Immune activation in hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) is not binary (hot vs cold).
- Instead, it reflects **multiple independent regulatory layers of immune activity**.

---

### 3. Immune Tolerance as a Reprogrammed State
- Immune tolerance is not a complete shutdown of immune function.
- It reflects a **reprogrammed immune equilibrium state**:
  - Effector functions are restrained
  - Regulatory mechanisms remain active
- Suggests tolerance is a **dynamic state shift rather than simple suppression**.

---

### 4. TCR Repertoire Structure vs Antigen-Driven Selection
- Some enriched TCR motifs lose significance after baseline-aware normalization.
- Indicates that part of the observed signal originates from **intrinsic repertoire architecture**, not antigen-driven selection alone.
- Highlights the importance of controlling for baseline structural bias in repertoire analysis.

---

### 5. CMV vs EBV Repertoire Dynamics
- CMV-associated repertoires:
  - Subtle, broadly distributed deviations
  - Consistent with chronic immune engagement and long-term adaptation
- EBV-associated repertoires:
  - More pronounced and selective deviations
  - Suggesting stronger antigen-driven clonal expansion
- Key distinction: **chronic immune shaping vs focused antigen-driven response**

---

## 🧠 Overall Perspective

Across these analyses, a recurring theme emerges:

> Immune responses in cancer and chronic infection are not binary states, but layered systems where activation, tolerance, and repertoire structure can be partially uncoupled.

This suggests a need for:
- multi-dimensional immune modeling
- baseline-aware repertoire analysis
- systems-level interpretation beyond single biomarkers
